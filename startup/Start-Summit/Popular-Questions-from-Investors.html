Questions Investors Ask Startups

Below is a comprehensive list of questions that investors (venture capitalists, angel investors, accelerators, and incubators) might ask startups during a pitch or due diligence process. These are categorized for clarity and include detailed sub-questions where relevant.

Founding Team & Leadership

  1. Who are the founders, and what are their professional and personal backgrounds?
  2. What specific experiences or skills make your team uniquely qualified to execute this idea?
  3. Have any of you started a company before? If so, what was the outcome?
  4. How did the team come together, and how long have you known each other?
  5. What motivates each founder to work on this venture?
  6. Are all founders committed full-time? If not, why, and what’s the plan to transition?
  7. How do you divide responsibilities among the team?
  8. How do you make decisions as a team, and how do you resolve conflicts?
  9. What’s the equity split among founders, and how was it determined?
  10. Are there vesting schedules in place for founder equity? If not, why?
  11. What gaps exist in your current team (e.g., technical, sales, operations)?
  12. How do you plan to recruit for those gaps (e.g., hires, advisors, co-founders)?
  13. Do you have advisors or mentors? Who are they, and what value do they add?
  14. Have any founders left the company already? If so, why?
  15. How do you plan to retain talent as the company grows?

Problem & Solution

  1. What specific problem are you solving, and why is it a pressing issue?
  2. Who experiences this problem, and how severe is it for them?
  3. How are people currently addressing this problem (existing solutions or workarounds)?
  4. What’s your solution, and how does it improve on what’s already out there?
  5. What’s your unique value proposition (UVP) in one sentence?
  6. Why hasn’t this problem been adequately solved yet (market gaps, technology limitations)?
  7. How did you validate that this is a real problem worth solving?
  8. What evidence do you have that customers want your solution (e.g., surveys, pre-sales)?
  9. Could your solution be applied to other problems or industries? If so, how?
  10. What risks exist in your solution not being adopted (e.g., behavior change required)?

Market Opportunity

  1. What’s your Total Addressable Market (TAM), Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM), and Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)? How did you calculate these?
  2. Who is your primary target customer (demographics, psychographics, firmographics)?
  3. How large is your target market in terms of revenue potential or user base?
  4. What’s the growth rate of this market, and what’s driving it?
  5. Who are your direct competitors, and how do you compare to them?
  6. Who are your indirect competitors (e.g., substitutes or alternatives)?
  7. What’s your competitive advantage or “unfair edge” (e.g., IP, network effects)?
  8. How defensible is your position against new entrants or copycats?
  9. Are there macroeconomic or industry trends that support your timing?
  10. What’s the biggest external threat to your market (e.g., regulation, tech shifts)?
  11. How fragmented or consolidated is your market, and how does that affect your strategy?

Product or Service

  1. What’s the current stage of your product (concept, prototype, MVP, fully launched)?
  2. Can you walk us through a demo or explain how it works in detail?
  3. What’s your product development roadmap for the next 6, 12, and 24 months?
  4. What technology stack or infrastructure powers your product?
  5. How do you ensure product quality and reliability?
  6. What feedback have you received from early users, and how have you acted on it?
  7. What’s your process for prioritizing new features or improvements?
  8. Are there any intellectual property protections (patents, trademarks) in place?
  9. What technical risks or challenges do you foresee (e.g., scalability, security)?
  10. Are there regulatory or compliance hurdles (e.g., FDA, GDPR)? How will you address them?
  11. How long did it take to build your MVP, and what were the key learnings?
  12. What’s your approach to user experience (UX) and design?

Business Model

  1. How do you generate revenue (e.g., subscriptions, sales, ads, licensing)?
  2. Why did you choose this business model over others?
  3. What’s your pricing strategy, and how did you determine it?
  4. How does your pricing compare to competitors?
  5. What’s your customer acquisition cost (CAC), and how do you calculate it?
  6. What’s your customer lifetime value (LTV), and how do you measure it?
  7. What’s your LTV-to-CAC ratio, and how does it trend over time?
  8. Are there opportunities for recurring revenue or upselling?
  9. How scalable is your revenue model as you grow?
  10. What are your gross margins, and how do they compare to industry benchmarks?
  11. Are there secondary revenue streams you plan to explore later?
  12. What’s your break-even point, and how long will it take to reach it?

Traction & Validation

  1. What traction do you have so far (e.g., users, revenue, downloads, partnerships)?
  2. What’s your month-over-month growth rate in key metrics?
  3. What’s been your most significant milestone or achievement to date?
  4. Can you share specific examples of customer success or satisfaction?
  5. Do you have any paying customers? If so, how many and how much revenue?
  6. What’s your churn rate, and how are you addressing it?
  7. Have you secured any notable partnerships or letters of intent?
  8. What’s your Net Promoter Score (NPS) or other customer satisfaction metric?
  9. How repeatable is your sales or acquisition process?
  10. What’s the biggest obstacle to accelerating your traction right now?

Go-to-Market Strategy

  1. What’s your go-to-market (GTM) plan, step by step?
  2. How do you plan to acquire your first 100 or 1,000 customers?
  3. What marketing channels will you prioritize (e.g., SEO, paid ads, content)?
  4. What’s your sales process (e.g., direct sales, self-serve, channel partners)?
  5. How long is your sales cycle, and how can you shorten it?
  6. What’s your customer onboarding process like?
  7. How will you retain customers and reduce churn?
  8. What’s your plan for scaling customer acquisition cost-effectively?
  9. Are you targeting a niche first, or going broad? Why?
  10. How will you measure the success of your GTM efforts?
  11. What’s your geographic focus (local, national, global), and why?

Financials & Funding

  1. What are your revenue projections for the next 1, 3, and 5 years?
  2. What are your key cost drivers (e.g., COGS, marketing, salaries)?
  3. What’s your current monthly burn rate, and how has it changed over time?
  4. How much cash do you have in the bank, and what’s your runway?
  5. How much capital are you raising, and what are the specific uses of funds?
  6. What milestones will this funding help you achieve?
  7. What’s your valuation, and how did you arrive at it?
  8. Have you raised money before? If so, how much, from whom, and at what terms?
  9. What’s your cap table look like (founders, employees, prior investors)?
  10. Are you offering equity, convertible notes, or SAFEs in this round?
  11. What’s your plan if you don’t hit your financial targets?
  12. Do you have financial models or forecasts you can share?
  13. What are the biggest assumptions in your financial projections?

Risks & Challenges

  1. What’s the biggest risk to your business, and how will you mitigate it?
  2. How will you handle competition that copies your idea?
  3. What happens if a key team member leaves?
  4. Are there legal or ethical risks tied to your product or industry?
  5. How will you adapt if market conditions change (e.g., recession, tech disruption)?
  6. What’s your contingency plan if this funding round fails?

Vision & Exit Strategy

  1. What’s your long-term vision for the company in 5-10 years?
  2. How do you see your company impacting the industry or the world?
  3. Are you building to sell, go public, or stay independent? Why?
  4. Who are potential acquirers for your business?
  5. What’s your ideal exit timeline, and what valuation do you aim for?
  6. How will you ensure the company scales without losing its core mission?
  7. What legacy do you want to leave with this venture?

Investor Fit & Ask

  1. Why are you pitching to us specifically? How do we align with your needs?
  2. Beyond capital, what value do you expect from us (e.g., network, expertise)?
  3. How much ownership are you offering for this investment?
  4. What’s your plan for future fundraising rounds?
  5. How involved do you want investors to be in your operations?
  6. What’s the minimum check size you’re accepting, and why?
  7. Are there other investors already committed to this round?

Miscellaneous

  1. What keeps you up at night about this business?
  2. What’s the most contrarian belief you hold about your industry?
  3. How do you stay motivated through setbacks?
  4. What’s one question we haven’t asked that you wish we would?
  5. How do you define success for yourself and the company?
  6. What’s the worst-case scenario, and how would you handle it?
